What is a palindromic DNA sequence?

The term palindrome refers to a sequence of characters which reads the same forward and backward. In genetics, palindrome takes on a different meaning when we compare the two strands of nucleotides included in a single molecule of DNA.

Each nucleotide is composed of one of four nucleobases, adenine (A), cytosine (C), guanine (G), or thymine (T), which binds to its complimentary pair on a second nucleotide strand. The nucleobase A always pairs with T, and C always pairs with G.

When the sequence of nucleobases is equal to its complimentary strand read backwards (see image below), it is said to be a palindromic DNA sequence.

Testing Tips

  • A sequence such as "tggatcca" is a palindromic sequence.
  • A sequence such as "tggacca" is NOT a palindromic sequence.
  • The checker is not case sensitive.
  • Spaces or characters other than the letters A,T,C, and G will return an error message.
